I am a PhD candidate at School of Information Technology, IIT Delhi under the supervision of Prof. Sumeet Agarwal and Prof. Rajakrishnan Rajkumar. During my doctoral studies, I spent ~3 years as a Senior Research Fellow (SRF) as well as Visiting Student Researcher (VSR) at the Department of Humanities and Social Sciences, IISER Bhopal. My PhD work focuses on Cognitive Modeling of Hindi Syntactic Choice Phenomena where I mainly develop cognitively motivated computational models to account for comprehension and production processes in Hindi. I am broadly interested in the mixture of computational psycholinguistics, natural language processing, machine learning, and cognitive science.

Before starting my grad school, I worked for Honeywell Aerospace, Bangalore as Senior Engineer where I developed softwares for testing electrical hardwares in cockpit systems viz., communication devices, display screens, and conducted automated Test Coverage Analyses. I recieved Dual Degree, B.Tech in Electrical Engineering and M.Tech in Information and Communication Technology from IIT Delhi. I took minor degree worth of courses for credit (20 credits) in Linguistics during my undergrad. As a part of my Master's Thesis, I worked on computational modelling of Hindi word order.
